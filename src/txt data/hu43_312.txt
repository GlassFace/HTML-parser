 @Teams sucking I think a lot of people need to realize that for the vast majority of players, skill is related to LoL to only a certain extent. Take, for example, three people in ELO hell: Person A: Baddie The baddie is the vast majority of people in ELO hell. He/she belongs here because he/she is just terrible at the game. Whether it be a lack of mechanical skills, horrible tactical decisions, or just all around general incompetence, he/she will never get out of ELO hell. Just awful. A lot of baddies confuse themselves for person B in my classification system. Person B: Good Player in Bad Division Occasionally, you come across a legitimately good player in a bad division. He/she will perform well nearly every game by winning lane, getting farm/kills, and making good plays all around the map. Unfortunately for this person, his/her team is too heavy, and accounting for only 20% of the team's ability is not enough. When all of the other members on his/her team **** the bed, there's little that can be done, despite the fact that the good player may be 6-1 with 210 farm at 20 minutes. This player is often told that it's his fault that he can't get into higher leagues, but a lot of the time it's not true. Going on tilt from bad team after bad team doesn't help either. Person C: the God of LoL This person is just passing through the division. I'm talking about players like Froggen, Faker, Wildturtle, or Cruiserthebruiser--all people I've seen grind from Silver 1 to Diamond 1 quickly. On Egg of Froggen, until about Diamond 2 Froggen was something along the lines of 26-0 on Fizz. He was so good at the game that he'd dominate his lane from 2 minutes in, and then dominate every other lane. Wildturtle is another example with being able to take four accounts to Challenger. He's just so incredibly good at the game that in teamfights he can legitimately outplay people in 1v5 and his teammates only need to act as meatshields and CC bots to let him do his thing. Unfortunately to deny the existence of person B, people often refer to person C. This is kind of silly because you can be a Plat 4 level player stuck in Gold 5; it's not that hard to believe. Of course if you're a top tier challenger player you're going to be able to smash through the early divisions solo. A player who's skilled above his league may just not be as good as Faker, putting him in a really bad spot. 
 Eh, from what I've seen it's more like "Baddie" being players who are sorely lacking in at least one area of their play you mentioned, who would be capable of transitioning to a "Good player" once they identify those flaws and actually address them. You only stay a baddie if you keep refusing to address your own inadequacies and claiming you're doing everything you can. For sure though with the current system, there's likely players who deserve to be in a higher division, if not a higher tier, but are simply unable to play so well they make up for all of their teams flaws. I don't those players are common though. I think a lot of people think they themselves are that player, because yeah maybe they farm like a maniac and got well ahead in their lane, but if they aren't making the right decisions throughout the game in how they handle their lane and how they interact with their other lanes then they aren't good enough to be moving up. If you're ahead by a couple waves worth of CS and up a kill or two in lane, you should probably be looking to gain global advantages (towers, dragon), steal from the enemy jungle, or gank your teams lanes so more people on your side are benefiting from the advantage you took. That's where I see a lot of "good players" failing to actually show that they are good players. Granted, sometimes just suppressing your lane opponent as long as possible to keep them from snowballing might be the right decision. If you're against an assassin mid, especially like Kassadin or something, or if you're against Jax or Singed top and can keep them from getting those early advantages that allow them to become such late game nightmares then you're doing well. But just sitting in lane up to 20 minutes, repeatedly killing your lane opponent and farming up a bunch while your other lanes are falling behind or at best going even is not necessarily going to win you the game. Farm and kills are just a means to an end. They don't win the game, at best they just potentially make it easier to take the actual objectives that lead to wins. If you aren't making the most of those advantages then you're part of the reason your team lost no matter how well you were doing.
