    @KT
    That's definitely a great idea. Generally speaking, that's what I normally try to do as a support and it's one of the biggest reasons why I prefer playing Sona, because she has the kit to do damage, the ability to stop big plays from occurring and also have the ability to create big plays. It's also why I have Janna in my back pocket, but don't generally use her because she doesn't have the damage.

    When you put yourself in a position where you're going to put more presence in the game, be aware that you will have to do that for the rest of the game which is actually harder to do if you're in any other role but support. It means you will have to expand your map awareness beyond those wards on the map and theoretically predict what the other team is going to do and still keep up with objective control and farm.

    You also have to keep in mind your team composition and openly think about active targets you should aim for as well as being a lot more open to communication and attempt to diffuse issues as fast as possible if they occur. If your team composition can't win at the late game, end the game quickly. If the other team is demoralized, there's still a good chance that if they have a Vayne or a Tryndamere, they'll beat you on Champion power alone.

    While not everyone is terrible at the game and obviously, most players know who to focus and what to go for, something that will happen a lot is you'll end up failing to identify proper threats.

    For example... if you're going against a Nasus + Vayne combo, obviously, the choice to beat is Vayne, correct?
    But how do you stop Nasus from using his Ult, charging through and dropping his CC on your DPS and then two-shotting them?

    Simply put, you can't.
    No amount of presence will stop a Champion like that, especially when you have a Vayne that can clean up and visa versa... a Nasus who can clean up... and that's just identifying two champions.

    Generally speaking, since I main Support, my only real option to stop that is to convert my resources into getting a Zeke's Herald, Mikael's Crucible or a Frozen Heart and refocus my efforts in pressuring Baron control so when they attempt it, we can pick off targets and end the game after a forced engage, if possible.

    If you were placed in that situation, what would you do?
    More likely than not, in-fighting can cause a game to end simply because the team can't pull back together, but this is just an example of what happens if Champion strength over powers that.

    Started giving Lucian a go...
    Instead of building him as an auto attacker, I build him like Blue Ez.

    I would've gone with a Trinity Force since overall, that would've augmented his kit, but with the removal of Icy, the overlap with the movement speed buff off of his W and the new Trinity Force didn't sit well for me.

    So I opted for burst damage trades with auto attack follow ups on his down time.
    Manamune, Ice Gauntlet, Berserker Grieves, Infinity Edge, Phantom Dancer/Blade of the Ruin King, Last Whisper

    Ultimately, his play is very hit and run, which is what hes kind of about in the first place. You hit with your W, your chase where possible. With Ice Gauntlet, you can permanently chase if need be and duel where possible. Infinity Edge to give him more of a kick, Phantom Dancer to increase his auto attack potential as well as boosting his Ult or Blade of the Ruin King for targets with a lot of HP and still boost your Ult... and Last Whisper because it shreds every one.

    If you want to go the auto attacker route, you can use the same normal builds for everyone else but you can use the new Frozen Mallet since now it has a bit more attack damage and it combos really well with his W, giving him endless chase potential.

    The one thing I will say about Lucian though is he doesn't play that well as an auto attacker since his attack speed is extremely low. He is a champion meant for burst dueling and you'll get more bang doing so and the fact that he can chase will really put your discipline to the test.

    Zer0, any champion in the game can be good as an Auto Attacker.

    Ez isn't an auto attacker by nature, the majority of his power comes from spamming his kit, which is kind of the point. He's not a true auto attacker like Vayne, Ashe and Caitlyn.

    In that sense, Corki, Lucian and Ez are in the same category in that they're skill based ADC's and are 100% fully utilized as such. It's why you're supposed to build a Sheen on them and it's why they have dashes that force them to go "all-in" or to "disengage". They're not the kinds of Champions you're supposed to sit there and auto attack with, they're the kind of Champions that do burst ADC Damage and Auto Attack while waiting for resets as well as for kiting.

    If a bruiser jumps on a Corki, Lucian or Ez, they can take them down while Ashe and Caitlyn can't really do that.

    Just remember, the kit is just as important as the items you get. Any champion in the game can be built as any role, but you build to maximize their kit.
