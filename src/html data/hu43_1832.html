    So I've pretty much mentioned this in every one of these threads, I guess I'm hoping by mentioning it noone's dumb enough to repeat my mistakes. Cherish the time you have with others.

    Back in highschool:
    I was on my way home one day after class when we came upon a car accident at this intersection. There was debris strewn all over the road, the vehicles that collided looked like one big mass that had fused together, it was really impossible to tell much of anything apart. There was blood everywhere, seeping out of the wreckage, all over the glass in the road, puddles slowly working their way toward the storm drains in the street. Emergency services was already on scene, and they were wrestling with a bloody tarp, trying to draw it over the wreckage. We sat there for a few minutes stuck in traffic, just watching this scene play out, and I turned to one of my buddies and said "sucks for them," meaning the people in the accident, and let out a little laugh.

    Next day we found out that several of the people killed in the crash were some of our close friends. Everything from how I interacted with them previously to what I said that moment at the intersection I absolutely regret. I've been through quite a bit, made many dumb and foolish mistakes, but this is the only thing I absolutely regret
