    The difference between a good thread and a bad thread

    Recently there has been an influx of new members on A4. This is great, as it makes the board a lot fresher, but unfortunately some of our new friends seem to be a bit unaware of thread making etiquette. While I wholeheartedly believe that some of you are really just too stupid to be capable of understanding or caring, some of you are but just don't know how yet. And that's no big deal! Let's go over a few of the big no-nos in thread creation.

    (1) Don't make unspecific or uninformative titles

    *Easy question...
    *What do you think about...
    *Does anyone else...

    As you can see, people who see this thread on the front page are going to be completely in the dark as to what it's about, and either not click it or just guess and enter the thread. A thread title called "Does anyone else..." may very well be a thread asking if you synchronize your orgasms to the drops in dubstep songs. We want to know what we're getting into before jumping into a thread.

    (2) Don't ask leading or loaded questions

    *Why is Obama such a bad president?
    *Do you think Microsoft is just a greedy corporation?
    *The Boston Marathon bomber: misunderstood freedom fighter?

    Leading and loaded questions forcefully insert your own opinion into the title and attempt to lead others to agree with you. This is a very easy way to destroy any chance of a decent discussion occurring because people are going to come into the thread with guns blazing. Try to ask non leading and non loaded questions when you start a new thread. For example, for the first thread, you could ask "What do you think about Obama's work as president?" For the second one, you can just say "How do you see Microsoft as a company in terms of customer treatment?" For the third one, you can just stick your genitals into an electrical socket.

    (3) Don't include your own opinion in the opening post

    *Are you for or against interracial marriage? I'm against it because it's wrong and immoral.
    *What's your favorite console? I think X-Box 360 is by far the best.

    Lastly, when you ask a question and then immediately shove your opinion into the discussion, you look like an *******. This board is not a place for you to just shout your opinion out and then leave threads. If you want to begin a discussion, start by creating an interesting discussion and showing both sides of the arguments, or just simply ask a question. By stating your opinion first, people who disagree are likely just going to counter your ideas instead of first outlining their argument. The argument has already been crushed under the weight of your work.

    In general, threads containing long opening posts full of horrible misinformed arguments and Youtube videos that are supposed to act as citations should be avoided.

    Thank you all for reading. Hopefully A4 can be a little better now.

    The person who makes the topic should present:

    1) Topic - A brief description of the subject.

    2) Discussion - A quick overview of factors/considerations pertaining to the subject. They can be expressed in different view points or just delineated forthright.

    3) Recommendation - Present an elementary position to begin the discussion (a belief or conclusion) or further refine the Discussion to facilitate conversation. Establish a rebuttable presumption of sorts, it doesn't have to be prima facie, but reasonable.

    4) Moderate - Guide the discussion as it evolves.
