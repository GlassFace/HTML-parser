Pokemon Origin

A reboot/retelling of the original Pokemon game, featuring Red and Green as the protagonist and rival. Much grittier and truer to the Pokemon manga Adventures in tone and direction.

Kyoukai no Kanata

A pair of high schoolers, a half youmu boy and a girl who can wield blood magic, wind up involved in super natural events occurring in their town.

Hajime no Ippo Rising

Long awaited new season of the well-known anime and manga, about a young boxer named Ippo rising up through Japan's ranks, as he aims for a match against his rival, Miyata.

Space Dandy

The Dandiest Man in Space, Dandy, is an alien hunter searching for undiscovered species with his robot assistant QT and a cat alien named Meow.

Samurai Flamenco

    The club I belong to just bought a few more and picked up one more myself.

    Boss Monster expansion - If you're a video gamer and you haven't seen Boss Monster then you should look for it. The box looks like an original NES game for the original Boss Monster game. The box for the expansion looks like an original Game Boy game box. Even the inside of the box has the little compartment holding the cards, just like the original Game Boy games did.
    It's a great game, though. You're a boss and you're objective is to kill heros as they try to get through your dungeon.

    Coup - If you like games where you get to go out of your way to make life miserable for the other players, then this ones for you.

    Firefly board game - Not for the faint of heart, give yourself 3 - 6 hours to play this one.

    Caverna - Created solely to put an end to the massively manipulated card system in Agricola, this is, for all intents and purposes, the same game. It uses the same turn system as Agricola. You have to farm crops, animals, and also excavate the caves to make spaces to build rooms.
    The rooms replace the cards from Agricola. Other than that, however, the victory point system works almost exactly the same. Alas, it will still take you 3 -5 hours to play this one.

    Slash - I consider this one of the highlights of 2014. It's like Apples to Apples in that it's a card game and that each card has the name of a fictional character or real person on it.
    The original intent of the game was to put people together based on ideal partners/mates, but you can apply any situation you want. As such, this game can be as kid friendly or non-kid friendly as the players like.
    What makes the game great, however, is that this time the controller isn't the one coming up with the reasons why they picked a particular card to win. EX: Marge Simpson needs a babysitter. Anyone reading this knows that this is very tall order to fill. You'd be holding 10 cards, each with a name on it. You pick the best one suited for the job. In order, starting with the player to the left of the controller, each player has 30 - 45 seconds to tell a story/make a pitch as to shy the controller should pick their card.
    Lots of interesting ideas, lots of laughs, and lots of fun. I highly recommend this one to anyone.
