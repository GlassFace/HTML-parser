It's basically just a smooth and silky stone that has no other use besides trading, but you probably have a greater chance of getting one of the more useful items, like some twinkling titanite, a titanite slab, or the demon's great hammer.

    Don't bother putting much more than 10 points into ADP. I had 20+ before respeccing and the difference is negligible. It only adds about 2 invincibility frames to your roll at 99 ADP too, unlike what we were lead to believe pre-release. Lots of people are starting to do tests to see just what exactly ADP does, and if it's worth putting any points into at all.

    You're mostly better off just putting points into vitality to increase your equip load, making it easier to equip more while remaining under 70% for the fast roll.

    Also, after 80 or so hours I completed my first playthrough last night. The endings was...well, you'll see it for yourselves. I don't know if I'm going to focus on ranking up in covenants (only done with sunbros so far) next or if I should just get started on my NG+ run right away.     Originally Posted by Ian the Korean
    Isn't ADP a way to reduce your hitbox size?
    Nope. It just increases the speed you use your estus flask (rather, decreases the time you stand there not doing anything after you drink it), makes you raise your shield faster, roll sooner after being hit, increases poise and resistances.

    It was supposed to do more, but a number of gameplay features didn't seem to make it to the full game that were seen in the beta, and some aspects of adaptability were some of those features (or were misrepresented, such as how we do gain invincibility frames on our roll from adp, but even after leveling it to 99 we only get 2 or so).
