Pokemon Origin

A reboot/retelling of the original Pokemon game, featuring Red and Green as the protagonist and rival. Much grittier and truer to the Pokemon manga Adventures in tone and direction.

Kyoukai no Kanata

A pair of high schoolers, a half youmu boy and a girl who can wield blood magic, wind up involved in super natural events occurring in their town.

Hajime no Ippo Rising

Long awaited new season of the well-known anime and manga, about a young boxer named Ippo rising up through Japan's ranks, as he aims for a match against his rival, Miyata.

Space Dandy

    I'm pretty torn about the upcoming queue system.

    On one hand it'll be nice to have considerably less fighting over roles during picks, and the fact that you can specify the strategy you're trying to execute is pretty interesting (Lyte said they were able to use it to queue for a 5 mid strat, which is delightfully insane). There doesn't seem to be any reason for the traditional meta to be locked unless that's what you want to play. I love the idea of being able to play the role I want to play rather than working through the awful pick/ban situation we're in now.

    On the other hand some measure of the game's spontaneity will be gone. If your team decides to change up the strategy while in the pre-game lobby how accommodating will this new system be? Also, systems like these produce their own special brand of annoyance. Anyone who's queued up in WoW's dungeon finder knows what I'm talking about, selecting a certain role places an enormous amount of expectation on your ability to play that role well. I worry that if you queue up to be a jungler in this new system and have a bad game the flak you get will be intensified because you queued up specifically to do that role and you should be amazing at it.

    Overall, I like the idea. I'm just worried about the new problems it'll introduce.

    Overall, I like the idea. I'm just worried about the new problems it'll introduce.
    Yes, but is that any different than calling or requesting a role in any other lobby? Assuming no one argues with you, and demands your requested role for themselves, there's not much of a difference that I can think of. If you call or request a role, you're expected to play it well. You're pretty much always expected to perform well, but if you're just filling because you have no other choice, you're not potentially taking a role away from someone else that thinks they could have done better (if they actually wanted to play your role, but let you since you called it or just took it because you had pick order on your side).

    As for teams possibly wanting to change what they want to do after getting into the lobby, I don't see why they couldn't select whatever setups they wanted and then play roles that they didn't queue into after getting into the game -- say a jungler and adc wanted to switch roles in the lobby for whatever reason, I don't see why the jungler couldn't choose an adc and the necessary runes/masteries, with the adc doing the same but with a jungler, and then playing those new roles after getting into the game. The new system is just to aid in queuing for the role you want for the most part, but it doesn't seem to restrict you from playing a different role after getting into the lobby/game should you need to for whatever reason.

    I'm really looking forward to it. I don't even care if it forever remains its own queue, and is never integrated into ranked (or given its own ranked queue). Just being able to play what I want when I want is enough for me.
