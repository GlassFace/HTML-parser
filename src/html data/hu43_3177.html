    Seiken Tsukai no World Break



    Seiken Tsukai no World Break (聖剣使いの禁呪詠唱) is a Japanese light novel series written by Akamitsu Awamura and illustrated by Refeia. SB Creative has published six volumes since November 2012 under their GA Bunko imprint. An anime television series adaptation by Diomedéa is scheduled to air from January 2015. A manga adaptation will be serialized in Kadokawa Shoten's Comp Ace magazine.

    The series takes place in a private high school involving the concept of "saviors." They are known as people who possess awakened memories of their past lives. The story tells of a young boy named Moroha Haimura who comes to this private school.

    At the school, there are two types of people: Saviors, who fight enemies with weapons and techniques gleaned from the Puraana powers from their own bodies, and Kuroma, who wipe out enemies with magic to manipulate the Maana powers that surpass physics. Moroha Haimura is the first person with past lives of both Shirogane and Kuroma.

    The main voice cast:
    Kaito Ishikawa as Moroha Haimura
    Ayana Taketatsu as Satsuki Ranjo
    Aoi Yuuki as Shizuno Urushibara
    Yui Ogura as Maya Shimon
    Maaya Uchida as Haruka Momochi

    I really do not see why people dislike it so much as far as being generic goes. I like it. It is way better and least generic out of the three major fantasy school life animes that I have seen this season--yes, I am looking at you, Fafnir and Absolute Duo. For an anime of this type, it is doing pretty well to avoid the usual generic subplots/devices, and for the things that it does different, I find it entertaining enough to like.

    The harem end actually seems canon rather than just one dominant girl with a bunch of vague loose ends for other girls. This is one of the main things that bother me about most harem animes in general, such as Chrome Shelled Regios. Furthermore, the relationships are at least founded on memories of past lives rather than just random love at first sight--especially if the girl was spotted naked for no reason, Fafnir and Blade Dance are good examples. That brings me to the next point.

    The ecchi part is not too over done. They are not trying to shove it in your face for no reason every chance they get. It is slightly more tame than what I have seen lately in most series.

    One of the best parts is that the main character is decently manly enough, especially when dealing with his love interests. "Imma bust through your wall and take off with ma waifu." Most other main characters are too shy or nervous to even touch their girls outside of extreme situations.

    Speaking of that, the main character is actually alpha enough in terms of abilities rather than just on par or below his harem with the occasional and situational leaps up.

    Loli can drive. She can be my getaway driver any day.

    That is all I can think of for now out of 5 episodes. Beach episode is an anime classic, so that does not even count.
