    Dubs > Subs, and here's why:

    If at all possible, I always try to stick to dubbed anime. I was waiting for Funimation to release to dub of Attack on Titan, then decided it was too long and started it anyways.

    And let me say this: AoT is a FANTASTIC anime. I've loved almost every minute of it. It's had me in more immersion and anticipation than an anime has done in a while.

    But it feels like I could be much more into it than I already am (which is legitimately difficult for me to percieve) and when I question why, this is the answer I come up with:

    It's not in English.

    Now, don't get me wrong; I do not mind at all listening to spoken Japanese. I think it's a beautiful language and I would definitely like to learn it someday. But that's the problem; I don't know it yet. I don't think it matters if it's in English, Japanese, or Pig Latin; any anime that's in a language I can understand gives me a better experience. This is probably why I still haven't finished SoRa No WoTo.

    If I can understand what they're saying instead of having to read it, the anime has the potential to feel so much more immersive and personal than the alternative. And yes, to a degree it is also less distracting and annoying.

    I can definitely deal with some subs (like AoT) if the anime is good enough (like AoT) to keep me wanting to watch it even though it's handicapped by subs; but ten times out of ten, I will say that dubbed anime has so much more potential to be fulfilling and exceptional than subbed.

    I finished Episode 8 of Attack on Titan tonight. Tomorrow, I will wake up, eat breakfast, and then watch Episode 9. I plan on finishing AoT subbed, because I don't have the option to watch it dubbed. But when Funimation releases the dubbed version, there's a chance I'll rewatch the anime (which is something I've never done before) just because I think AoT's current greatness (which, I will restate, is abundant) can be expanded all that much more if I understand what the characters are saying rather than reading it.

    What's your guys' opinion?

    It is the same to me whether I am reading or listening. However, it is hard to get immersed when the character and the voice are so repulsive of each other that it pushes you away with explosive force. When a character and their voice do not match very well, it becomes more of a distraction for me, and I am thinking, "stop. talking." I find this problem to be notorious in localized anime with few exceptions (and few reversed exceptions). For example, using an older woman's voice for a girl that would pass as a loli.

    I do not require the VA to be perfectly good and passionate. All I require is that the voice and character match. While certainly Japan has bad voice acting/actors as well, but I seldom hear any voices that I do not like.

But I think dubbing is far more restrictive and you lose a lot more since you have to choose certain words to sync the mouth or shorten the intended meaning further.
    There's that, and as mentioned, the seiyuu matching the character is a big deal to me. But as the above mentioned, the seiyuu typically tries too much. :o

    Also, you don't really read while watching subs. If you sit away a proper distance (normal distance from the screen), you don't have to move your eyes and you can see the whole subbed line at once and your brain will take in that information and process it. Well, if you are comfortable with the English language at least. In fact, I use my peripheral vision and I still understand the subs. Only when the subbing takes 2 lines would I have to actually read it--which depends on the release.
