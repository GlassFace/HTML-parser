    So I wasn't really impressed enough to spring for anything after HG/SS, but X/Y seems to be shaping up into a great game. There seem to be some natural progressions (mega evolutions, "horde encounters," super training, photo spots in game etc. Then there are some things that are long overdue and I'm specifically talking about the "player search system."

    from pokemonxy.com:

    Connect and play with other Trainers at any time with the new Player Search System (PSS), which allows you to find other people playing Pokmon X and Pokmon Y! You'll connect to other players nearby or around the world through the Internet. You can even communicate with players you've never had any contact with before.

    All you need to do to communicate with other players on the PSS is to tap their icons. Then you can easily choose to trade or battle with them. That's not all the PSS can do, so look forward to more info on this exciting feature in the future!

    You're always connected to the world with the PSS! You can connect not only to players nearby, but also to players all around the world.

    Using the PSS, you can do a variety of things, starting with trading and battling Pokmon. But as you'll see, there's much more to the PSS!
    Sounds like they're expanding on the social aspects of the game which is fantastic. It'll be interesting to see how Nintendo handles an online system that allows players to interact with people they don't actually know.

    As a fan of the first couple of generations my questions are:
    1.) How often will I run into the older pokemon generations?
    2.) Will every pokemon be available to catch or trade with just these two games(X/Y)? (meaning that I don't have to own a copy of Pokemon:Pearl just to get a Glaceon)

    My problems with pokemon have been the neglect of the social experience, given what the game is about and our online capabilities, and having to either own every game or knowing someone who has the right equipment to actually catch/trade all the pokemon.
    They don't have to burn the books, they just remove them.
