    I'm currently watching Mushishi, just finished episode 13 not too long ago; so I've past the halfway mark on it. Funny how quickly an anime I really engage with goes by so fast. I find every aspect of their series fascinating, from the art to the music that sets the atmosphere of the scenes to philosophical and thematic concepts each episode delves into.

    I'm going to be completely honest, while there are many episodes I can completely wrap my head around in terms of having some sort of interpretation in regards to looking at it metaphysically as opposed to taking everything literally at face value, there are some episodes I have nothing; in others words, a small number of episodes that I don't completely get. No worries though because it gives it replay value; that's what revisiting such shows is for.

    What I do like about this series is that a person can take everything on it literally or metaphysically and it works either way; this is different from viewing Kino's Journey where the vast majority of the series should be seen allegorically. This is by no means a knock against Kino's Journey as it is a great anime that I think everyone should take a look at as is the case with Mushishi.

    In that regard, Mushishi reminds me a lot of Kino's Journey in many ways in terms of the whole episodic travelling protagonist and art vibe, but, also Mermaid Forest for some strange reason; it does have that vibe to it as well. Perhaps it's because the premise of Mushishi is based more on traditional Japanese Folklore similarly to Mermaid Forest and has pretty much nothing in terms of western influence to it as is the case with a lot of other anime.

    With that, all I have to say for now is that I really like hearing Travis Willingham where he's utilizing a softer, more mellow tone to his voice; it's almost enough to lull a person to sleep, but then again, while Mushishi, does have some unsettling aspects to its presentation, it's also very mellow and somewhat soothing.
