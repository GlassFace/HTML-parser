Valvrave the Liberator Season 2

The second season of laser space vampire high schoolers piloting suicidal mechs against space-nazis and space-USA.

Kill la Kill

Ryuko is searching for her father's murderer, her only clue half of a pair of giant scissors. She transfers into Honnoji Academy, a dystopian high school ruled by the student council who possess Goku Uniforms; uniforms imbued with super powers.

Pokemon Origin

A reboot/retelling of the original Pokemon game, featuring Red and Green as the protagonist and rival. Much grittier and truer to the Pokemon manga Adventures in tone and direction.

Kyoukai no Kanata

A pair of high schoolers, a half youmu boy and a girl who can wield blood magic, wind up involved in super natural events occurring in their town.

Hajime no Ippo Rising

Long awaited new season of the well-known anime and manga, about a young boxer named Ippo rising up through Japan's ranks, as he aims for a match against his rival, Miyata.

Space Dandy

The Dandiest Man in Space, Dandy, is an alien hunter searching for undiscovered species with his robot assistant QT and a cat alien named Meow.

Samurai Flamenco

An eccentric male model decides to become a super hero in order to become a hero of justice, despite having no super powers.

    1) DB + DBZ + yes, even DB GT
    Everybody hates on GT, but it was comical and entertaining, and Gogeta is a bamf so mehhh

    2) One Piece
    I didn't want to pick a bunch of well known anime but I can't help it with the ones I have and will pick. They all appeal to my sense of humor, morals, and fascination with badassery

    3) Bleach
    DBZ was my first anime but this is the one that made me fall in love with anime. Johnny Yong Bosch is a boss Ichigo. I even watched the subs when I got impatient, even though I normally stick to dubs. Yeah the anime original story arcs kind of sucked in comparison to the manga arcs, but they weren't that bad...just in comparison

    4) Ghost In the Shell (whole series)
    Terrific. Not a whole lot else to say about it. Cyborgs, cyber-brains, futuristic crimes, and lots of poignant philosophical arguments. What's not to love?

    5) The Future Diary
    I've said it before, I'll say it again. It is the most insane and perverse love story ever imagined. Yuno Gasai is the most adorable yandere character of all time, and despite being gory and somewhat ridiculous it manages to stay a very endearing tail of two tragically star crossed lovers.

    That's a spontaneous 5, the order and picks would probably change if I was in a different mood. I liked Soul Eater despite the manga story line being butchered, Sword Art Online was and will continue to be fantastic (I've only seen the dub), Naruto has it's moments but the filler episodes are usually unbearable, and it definitely has it's lulls. Oh yeah, Rock Lee and his Ninja Pals can efffofff.

    Honorable mentions: Code Geass, Cowboy Bebop, Gundam Wing, Fairy Tail, Yu Yu Hakasho

    By far the hardest anime to leave out of my top 5. Hunter x Hunter, the first one (after all I usually stick to dubs). It starts out somewhat slow, but by the end you will have born witness to some of bamfiest of the bamfest underage characters in the history of anime (or in my case dubbed anime).

    1. Monster - A young japanese doctor in germany decides to save the life of a boy over the town's mayor, costing him a prominent position in his hospital. The boy, it turns out, is actually a horrific serial killer the doctor is determined to track and stop.
