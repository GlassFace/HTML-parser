    If offered, Would you accept immortality?

    Well, not really immortality.

    Here's the situation.
    A mage approaches you and shows you a book.
    If you sign the cover, you will become immortal.
    You cannot die, You can regenerate from a single cell to your current appearance. You will not grow old.

    The ONLY way that you can die is after 900,000 years then yourself will accept death if you somehow grow bored of living.

    OR you can immediately accept death and you will instantly cease to exist.

    So what is it?

    When there are only those 2 choices then yes.

    The only issue with immortality is that you will eventually grow bored of living, as everything around you may eventually die, crumble, age etc., but you would not. It would almost be like your body is in suspended animation in a world where everything goes through their natural lifespans except yourself. It may be intriguing and even enjoyable at first but, when friends, relatives etc, begin to die and other such changes, I'd imagine it won't seem so exciting as it first appeared.

    As for the deal itself, I'd take it. Considering an ultimatum like that was proposed in the "deal", most people would apparently take the obvious choice of living for at least 900,000 years.

    But I have a question out of curiosity, OP. If you did decide to continue living after that 900,000 years was up, what happens then? Would you have another fixed time limit and still have the option to die, for a 2nd time?
