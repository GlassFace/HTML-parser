    Be glad it's not as bad as they said it was going to be in NY. Try living just outside Boston and see what being buried alive means.

    Many of the previous snow records for Boston were broken yesterday, and 3 more inches fell last night. Today and Wednesday are supposed to be sunny, which is good as the sun will go to work and help clear the roads, however, the next storm, which hits on Thursday night, is expected to dump another foot of snow inside 12 hours. The word blizzard was mentioned on the news. The good news is that, if Thursday is a blizzard then it may alter the weather pattern to keep the snow away from New England for a while. If it doesn't, then there's another storm on Sunday going into Monday that will dump at least a foot on the region.

    If you haven't had to deal with snow, I'm happy for you. Be glad you haven't. Here's the current situation in Boston:

    MBTA (Boston's public transportation system): Commuter and subway rails shut down indefinitely. Limited bus service available.
    Parking Bans in place for Boston and many of the cities across the state.
    State of Emergency was declared by governor Baker last night.
    Businesses, although open, are suffering badly as people can't get to them.
    The mounds of snow on the street (which are eating up all the parking spaces) are now over 7 feet tall. There is, literally, no place left to put the snow.
    Snow removal trucks are taking the snow off of major streets but the side streets are atrocious.
    If you're stuck here, worry about your own sidewalk and driveway before you worry about someone elses. Clear yours out first, then help the neighbors.
    If you're like me, and your back, after shoveling snow for the last 2 weeks, has said enough is enough, then take advantage of the kids looking to make some cash by shoveling. I've already given those kids $120 to shovel out the sidewalk and driveway 3 times, and it looks like they may make another $120 before the weekend is out.
    The artic air isn't going anywhere anytime soon, so the snow can't melt. And I shudder to think what will happen when it does start to melt (storm drains are buried. There's no place for the water to go).
    As I am, so shall ye be.
