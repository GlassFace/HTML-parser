    The only thing wrong with the release timing of the new generation of consoles was the limited number of new generation of games. Most games for the PS4 and X1 were already available for the PS3 and 360, and while there are marked improvements to those games on the new consoles, they're not new, and not terribly good reasons to get the new consoles so soon. The next 2-3 months will change that, but at release there just wasn't a lot to offer that didn't already exist.

    That said, I got a PS4. I really wanted to play Battlefield 4 and Assassin's Creed 4: Black Flag, but I felt like I would be wasting my time and money getting them for the PS3 when I planned on getting a PS4 no matter what anyhow, so I just got a PS4 and those games for it (in the case of BF4 it was a wise choice, as despite all of the game's problems across all systems, it runs like complete **** and will forever do so on the PS3 and 360). I don't regret it at all. Love the games, BF4 especially, love the controller to death, and love the PS4's XMB, which is slick as **** (aside from one minor irritant that may be changed in a future update). I have a lot to look forward to, as well.

    I've still been playing my PS3 more lately, as obviously I only have two PS4 games (and two PSN games: Resogun and Don't Starve: Console Edition), and I've been chugging through a backlog of PS3 games. PS4 isn't going anywhere, though.

    When you install Titanfall, it decompresses the audio off of the disc and stores it on your hard drive.
    For performance, it was actually faster for Respawn to stream the audio completely raw. Basically, what they were saying is that instead of using extra processing to decompress those files on the fly, they felt it was better served for other elements such as graphics and game play.

    --

    To be quite honest, Titanfall isn't that great. It's a fun game and all, and it deserves the review scores it gets, but generally speaking, the only mode that will keep the game afloat in a "eSport" setting is Titan Last Stand. There is virtually no strategy involved in any other mode and what makes Titan Last Stand so fun is the amount of communication required along with skill and teamwork. If you ever played a match of Counter-Strike, that single mode brings the mentality of Counter-Strike into it.

    The other problem with Titanfall is not allowing players to have their own servers. The game will most likely be short lived and Respawn squashed most player's ability to make modifications so you can't have an eSport scene if you wanted to since you can't exactly queue a team against another team by choice.
