    While a "like" or "+1" option has proven to be ineffective in the past I actually like the idea of a simple "Agree" mechanic on the forum.

    I think one of two things generally happen on the forum, especially in "heavy" sections like GNAD, PLD, and CE:

    1. The discussion gets swept up with only a few people making big posts as they try to make their points and counter others. This can get unwieldy and difficult to get in on.
    2. Someone nails a point that the reader wanted to make and their replying to it would only be repeating that point. Often times when this happens people seem to back off the discussion or simply reply with "I agree" and not offer much more.

    The ability to simply "agree" alone could add to a thread as people begin drawing battle lines. Not only can readers show their support for arguments that they agree with but it gives the people who are doing the arguing a sign that they might be on to something if they're getting "agrees".

    Most importantly, something like this could help reduce spammy posts. And you can't deny that when someone +anything's something you've done it feels great (and it can feel great to +anything someone!).

    Edit: After thinking about it I think just having the "agree" and not the "disagree" would be best. Agrees would contribute positivity (for the most part) while disagrees would only contribute negativity.

    The idea of having an Agree/Disagree system or even simply just an Agree button is nice but ask yourself what is the ultimate point of it? Even if no one can "disagree" with someone else or their post, that doesn't mean it will not share some of the same problems that we had with the Reputation system or any other similar system.

    The Reputation system was abused by people who would simply down vote someone because they were jealous, malicious, or simply didn't like the person but removing the ability to "punish" someone doesn't remove the idea of score-keeping which was a large problem. People will still compete and judge people based solely on how many "Agrees" someone has. Plenty of people will claim that they wont but it is human nature to evaluate someone else in relation to yourself. People will always look for some way to compare themselves to others and we have had multiple systems in the past that allowed this but have cut back to having just a post count and join date which people still use to compare themselves to other users.

    A4 is not Reddit or some other message board built on the idea that the most liked or popular threads remain on the front page without contribution outside of a single mouse-click. This is a forum that encourages discussion, a thread is popularized because people participate within the thread and further the discussion, not just by clicking an "Agree" button while the chaff falls to the back pages because they are not worthy of discussion.

    A4 has endured a lot of changes but in the end we have realized that above all else this is a forum for people with similar interests to engage each other in discussion. Applying layers of superficial systems that don't add anything to that experience, more often than not actually hinder it in direct and indirect ways. There are some of us still here that have been around for a while and we have been through many of these systems that were eventually removed because they don't reinforce the concept that A4 was created for. In hindsight, I wonder how many people would still be here today if we had chosen not to implement many of those ineffectual systems in the first place.
