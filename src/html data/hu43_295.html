    Originally Posted by Pured

    Yeah like Struggler said, it just doesn't really mean much on the competitive level if you don't work well together. Sure, getting to challenger is a good indication that you are mechanically competent, but it doesn't really say much about what your champion pool is, assuming you aren't one of the numerous people who simply rode a single champion to the top like that dude who made proxy singed popular, it doesn't say anything about your ability to work with four other people given the ridiculously stupid **** people do in soloqueue at all levels, and it doesn't indicate you would be mature enough to move into a gaming house and actually put in the time and effort required to make a team work. Think about the strategy teams put into researching their opponents for picks and bans, lane matchups, and even just communicating more closely with the jungler between lanes to set up ganks. In soloqueue all of that stuff is pretty much non-existent and at best you ward and throw out some pings.

    I mean yeah, if you're recruiting players for a team you probably want people in D1 or challenger as your minimum requirements, but simply being at that level isn't much of an accomplishment if your goal is playing professionally. NA focuses primarily on soloqueue and isn't able to compete meaningfully on a world level, whereas Asian teams have dedicated backup teams to scrim against. There's tons of problems with the way NA as a region approaches the pro scene and soloqueue is a major part of it.
