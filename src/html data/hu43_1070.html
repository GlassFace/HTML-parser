    Lol I love that I have been mentioned twice in this thread, that's pretty awesome, go me!

    Anyway, I feel theres a lot to address when talking about open.poly relationships. so I think I'll start with definitions and examples so everyone is on the same page =3

    Primary partner/s, usually a spouse or most important partner/s in a group. Often in stable triads, everyone is considered a primary.
    Secondary/tertiary partners, usually these partners are not as high of a priority as the primary/ies. Usually extramarital partners, or flings, may also be LTR or LDR partners.
    Open = Sex with multiple partners, there may or may not be a primary partner.
    Poly = sex and emotional connections with others, these don't always have to be LTR but they can be.
    Swinging = Sex and no emotional attachment with others, there may be a primary and there are different levels of swinging depending on the people involved.


    As for myself I prefer polyamory, neither my husband or I are interested in sleeping around, but we like the freedom to pursue a relationship that seems worth our time if we choose.
    We aren't the jealous types, and we each understand that other people offer, not a better relationship experience, but a different one altogether. We know that as people mature (and we have been together for 9 years now, so that's a good chunk of time) they may want different things from a relationship, and it's not that the other partners doesn't want to provide it, they just may not be able to.
    Or to put it more simply, neither of us expects the other to be everything we need at any given time. Sure they can come close, and they might fit perfectly from time to time, but to expect that consistently seems a bit unrealistic and selfish to us. Having the freedom to pursue other opportunities takes a lot of stress out of our relationship, since we don't feel totally stuck with each other. Is it for everyone? Of course not, but when it works, it can work beautifully =3
    Since opening our relationship we have become much closer and appreciate each other much more because we get to see what other people are like, and what they bring to a relationship. All in all I think it's been one of the best things we have done for each other.

    Now there are also plenty of really terrible reasons to get into an open relationship, and I think boredom is one of the biggest. It's like having a baby to save a marriage. Instead of concentrating on how to fix, or dissolve their partnership in a positive way, they are adding whole new stressors to the system. Making the whole thing even more likely to fall apart horribly. It's especially bad when people rush into it without setting rules and boundaries first.

    Does everyone know their boundaries right out of the gate? Usually not, that's something you have to do some self searching to discover, and even sometimes you won't know what they are until they have been crossed. Though fall out from that kind of thing can be minimal if both partners are willing to work with the new information in a constructive way.

    Open relationships take a lot of work, and communication skills are a huge asset should you decide to try one, and they aren't for everyone for sure, though I'd like to hope I was able to open a few minds with this post and get people to see it's not all about sex, and trying to have your cake and eat it too =3

    Feel free to ask me any questions if I didn't cover something you were curious about.

    Also Just to add, I don't know who told you finding partners for open relationships is easy. Maybe if it's a lady looking for a dude for a fling or NSA deal, but otherwise? It can be just as difficult, if not more so then trying to find a partner when you are single XD
