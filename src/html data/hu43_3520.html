    Actually, I found how immersion works: an imbalance in the brain.

    Even if you aren't consciously hyper active, the fact is we think too much.

    Why is that? Because we never considered "tapping" our way to restful bodies.

    The solution is easy as pie: "Tapping" is essentially the best warmup ever. It even makes doing this a priority over boring stretching.

    If you're planning on binging on a game and getting yourself immersed, then don't exercise (for those that don't have hyperactive thinking). Exercise leads to reduction in self consciousness because you become conscious of your hands the moment you warm up into them.

    Just as some people have to listen extremely loud music to enjoy their day.

    If you get tired from a game and still want to do something, here's the solution: Tap your fingers and toes and speak whatever comes to mind first with your mouth slowly, then increase if you naturally want it and you will find it natural to speak good language. You'll eventually get used to using your mouth, and then you'll feel better because you're not second thinking your actions because your body is now in perfect harmony.

    Whenever I feel bored of a game, it's because I've too much energy in my head. And also, if you think too much, pictures become boring and words become fun.

    For the perfect immersion, you should balance yourself in both the primitive and neocortex, our thinker.

    Thinking-->Story Reading
    Tapping and exercise-->Looking at pictures without second thought.

    A lot of people have this imbalance that equals being uncomfortable. And a lot of people do so much thinking in the day that, when they look at computer screens, they don't consider "forcing yourself to exercise" will actually cause blockage in your veins because they can't accept anymore.

    Think of it as a computer. A computer needs to start up, so you need start up as well.

    Makes me think why I'm so smart compared to my parents: too much energy blockage.

    True power is not defined by comparison but by discovery of the unexpected. Just like we all laugh at the unexpected, discovery is also every person's best motivator. That is one angle of being self-accomplished. Basically becoming stronger than yourself.
