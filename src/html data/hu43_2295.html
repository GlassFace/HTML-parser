    A New Jersey teenager claiming that her mother and father tossed her out of their home and cut her off financially is using them for immediate support, current private-school fees and future college tuition. The parents, meanwhile, say that daughter Rachel Canning, 18, moved out voluntarily after refusing to abide by their rules.
    “We love our child and miss her. This is terrible. It’s killing me and my wife,” Rachel's father, Sean Canning, a town administrator and retired police officer, tells the Daily Record. “We have a child we want home. We’re not Draconian and now we’re getting hauled into court. She’s demanding that we pay her bills but she doesn’t want to live at home, and she’s saying, ‘I don’t want to live under your rules.’” The rules, he notes, include reconsidering her relationship with a boyfriend who may be a bad influence, being respectful, and abiding by her curfew. He and his wife, Elizabeth, who live in suburban Lincoln Park, about 25 miles outside of New York City, have kept their daughter’s car because they paid for it, says Canning, and he admits that they did stop paying Rachel's tuition at the private Morris Catholic High School. A hearing is scheduled to take place on Tuesday in the Morris County Superior Court.

    I don't like the spin on the article or this thread. The way federal funds for college is set up, is horrible for young kids who have rich parents who aren't willing to help them out.

    When you fill out a FASFA for college you have to include your parent's financial information unless you're 24 years old or have a child or are/have been in the military. That being said if she's already in private school her parents are obviously wealthy enough meaning more than likely she will get denied and federal aid-grants and loans. The only other option is going with high interest private loans from banks which an 18 year old with no credit history obviously won't get approved for without a co-signer which she might not even have.

    If I were in her situation I would feel cornered and desperate. It's not as easy as 'work hard, it's your responsibility, get over it and do it' and it's naive for everyone who thinks it is. Now that I'm going back to college for a second degree I have to use financial aid and despite my mother living in another state, me having a full time job with the state, and claiming independent on my taxes I still have to put her tax information on the FASFA meaning I'm only eligible for 5,500 USD a fiscal year which isn't enough to attend a public college for more than one semester a year.
