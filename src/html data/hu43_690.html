    It definitely does have more usefulness on a laptop/hybrid device because in that scenario you don't end up with the dreaded "gorilla arm" that comes from holding your arm up to touch a touchscreen monitor.

    That said, I've been using one at work for almost two years now and there are times when it's useful and times when it's just novelty. Particularly on Windows 8, some apps - a lot of games especially - are basically impossible to play or use effectively without touch, though if you actually want to do stuff like that you'd want to invest in one of the newer touch monitors which support being moved around at all sorts of angles so you can almost lay them down completely for things. You'd also have to make sure you get one that supports capacitive touch and has at least five-finger touch support.

    The tl;dr version of all this is that you'll have to spend a lot of money on one. We're talking at least in the $500+ range, which at that point I'd say just get a Surface if you want to see what Windows 8 is all about on a touch screen (On a device it was made for, no less).
