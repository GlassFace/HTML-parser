Pokemon Origin

Kyoukai no Kanata

A pair of high schoolers, a half youmu boy and a girl who can wield blood magic, wind up involved in super natural events occurring in their town.

Hajime no Ippo Rising

Long awaited new season of the well-known anime and manga, about a young boxer named Ippo rising up through Japan's ranks, as he aims for a match against his rival, Miyata.

Space Dandy

Samurai Flamenco

    Meme culture

    Okay I'm just going to jump straight to the point: I'm sick and tired of memes. Just the idea of a meme makes me roll my eyes. Most memes are like huge bandwagons waiting for every person on the internet to jump on. I see people throwing these memes into random conversation just trying to be funny when it really makes no sense at all. Even worse, when people adapt a meme phrase into their every day vernacular. I should stop myself before I continue ranting because I'd like to hear what you all have to say C: How do you guys feel about memes like advice animals, rage faces and harlem shake? Do you reference memes a lot or would you rather have your own sense of humor? If you want you can check out the video below to get a better understanding of the point I'm trying to come across.
