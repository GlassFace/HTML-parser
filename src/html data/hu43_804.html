    I think your set up is fine. However, you may want to cut back on the processor, and upgrade the graphics card or get an SSD and HDD combo. It depends on what you intend to do the most with it. If gaming, a better gpu will give you more noticeable results. If you are mostly using it for graphics and video editing and transcoding, then a better CPU and SSD combo will serve you better. Do be careful of benchmarks. Most benchmark results may look nice, but they tend to not be noticeable in real world usage.

    As for cooling, you could always just put a bucket of water or ice in front of your fans and get good thermal paste.

    It is my golden rule to never jump at a new operating system. I like to wait at least a year for developers to work out bugs and increase compatibility. I would suggest hanging onto win 7 for now and upgrade later.

    The machine is fine, talking to the software, it's a matter of preference: really the major change in Windows 8 that is a big negative to people is no start button (Instead replaced with the 'start screen'; all the other start button functionality, like searching, etc. are also now the start screen). I mean you can generally keep yourself in 'desktop land' and generally avoid most of the new interface if you want, but you will have to see it anytime you'd have used a start menu in the past.

    Speaking to the positives: In terms of compatibility, it's actually compatible with more hardware than Windows 7 is (Though it likely shouldn't matter either way given the components you're getting are almost certainly fine with both), so it's much more rare that you have to do things like hunt around for drivers online or pop in driver CDs. Not only that, the general performance is faster than Windows 7 - booting up is faster, restarting/shutting down is faster, moving files is faster, CPU and memory usage are generally better for most things: http://usabilitygeek.com/windows-8-v...mance-testing/

    So there are trade-offs - if you can stand the new interface you're rewarded with a generally faster and more efficient OS, but a lot of people can't get past the fact that it looks different.
