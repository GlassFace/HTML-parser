Valvrave the Liberator Season 2

The second season of laser space vampire high schoolers piloting suicidal mechs against space-nazis and space-USA.

Kill la Kill

Ryuko is searching for her father's murderer, her only clue half of a pair of giant scissors. She transfers into Honnoji Academy, a dystopian high school ruled by the student council who possess Goku Uniforms; uniforms imbued with super powers.

Pokemon Origin

A reboot/retelling of the original Pokemon game, featuring Red and Green as the protagonist and rival. Much grittier and truer to the Pokemon manga Adventures in tone and direction.

Kyoukai no Kanata

A pair of high schoolers, a half youmu boy and a girl who can wield blood magic, wind up involved in super natural events occurring in their town.

Hajime no Ippo Rising

Long awaited new season of the well-known anime and manga, about a young boxer named Ippo rising up through Japan's ranks, as he aims for a match against his rival, Miyata.

Space Dandy

The Dandiest Man in Space, Dandy, is an alien hunter searching for undiscovered species with his robot assistant QT and a cat alien named Meow.

Samurai Flamenco

An eccentric male model decides to become a super hero in order to become a hero of justice, despite having no super powers.

    Yeah I thought the way they animated Mako when she was getting ready for school was an amusing choice. The ridiculous faces are intentional and again, I don't see the "bad" animations you're suggestion; maybe you could pause it and give me some times within the episode that you think are bad? I mean for sure it's a TV anime, so there's going to be an occasional in between frame that isn't perfect, but the animation is incredibly smooth.

    I can see how it wouldn't appeal to everyone, but given how much they're borrowing from/inspired by 70s anime (Cutie Honey, Mazinger Z, literally anything by Go Nagai) I appreciate those kinds of touches. I don't really think it's fair to call it bad simply because it doesn't appeal to you when there's obviously a ton of effort being put into the show to get a specific effect.

    It reminds me of that early episode of TTGL where they had a guest director who went for a different art style and got a ton of flack from the fans who thought it looked "bad" when it simply didn't appeal to their expectations. And I think that's fine, when it comes to art people's tastes are going to vary pretty wildly. But this isn't like say, an episode of Naruto or Bleach where you simply have some really horrific in between frames every couple of seconds that have poorly or incorrectly drawn characters due to a lack of effort and attention. There's a stylistic choice at work here that was very intentional.

    Also, I'm perfectly fine with people critiquing the show for the things that don't appeal to them and discussing that. For example I wish there was less of the blatant fan service even if it is in line with their usual amount. Expressing that the style doesn't appeal is also perfectly fine, there's been some shows I couldn't get into due to the stylistic choices or artists involved. I just want more than "it's animated badly" is all, so I do appreciate people putting effort into their responses.
